# Pain Points

Collect the concrete complaints the post should answer.

## Good pain points
- permission confusion
- blocked auth flows
- unclear source of truth
- fragile automation
- brittle handoffs
- duplicated state
- unreadable output

## Evidence bar
- Prefer user-provided notes, forum complaints, support threads, issue trackers, reviews, logs, or direct product behavior.
- If a pain point is inferred, label it as an assumption.
- Do not turn a broad market trend into a reader pain unless you can name the failure it causes.

## Weak pain points
- "users want better tools"
- "AI is changing everything"
- "productivity is hard"

## Ask
- What is failing?
- Who feels the failure?
- What does it cost?
- What have they already tried?
- Why didn't that work?
- What did the writer see, try, break, learn, or get annoyed by?

## Author stake
- Keep first-person evidence when it exists.
- Do not translate "I kept reconstructing the work" into "users experience workflow inefficiency."
- If the post is personal, the author stake is part of the evidence, not decoration.

## Gate
Do not package the topic until the failure, cost, and attempted workaround are clear. For personal posts, also name the author stake or mark it missing.
