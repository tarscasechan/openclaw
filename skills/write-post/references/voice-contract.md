# Voice Contract

Decide who is speaking before shaping the post. This prevents clean structure from becoming impersonal.

## Choose the stance

- `I`: lived experience, personal lessons, process notes, mistakes, taste, or ownership.
- `you`: reader pain, practical advice, direct diagnosis, or builder-facing posts.
- `we`: shared industry problem, team practice, or a real collective frame.
- mostly third-person: reporting, neutral explanation, or product documentation.

## Include

- speaker: who owns the claim
- reader: who should feel addressed
- stake: why this matters to the speaker
- pronoun stance: `I`, `you`, `we`, or third-person
- texture: one concrete scene, frustration, or detail to preserve

## Gate

The voice contract passes when the post can answer:

- Who is talking?
- Who is being addressed?
- What lived friction or stake makes this worth writing?
- Which pronouns should appear naturally?

If the answer is only "users have a problem," the voice is not specific enough.
