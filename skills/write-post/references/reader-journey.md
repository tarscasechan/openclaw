# Reader Journey

Translate the internal Want/Need/Get brief into a natural post shape. The reader should never feel the scaffold.

## Beats

1. **Hook**: one short sentence that opens tension.
2. **Recognition**: show the reader the pain or scene they recognize.
3. **Friction**: name what keeps failing and what it costs.
4. **Turn**: reveal the useful reframe or mechanism.
5. **Mechanism**: explain how the new shape works.
6. **Tradeoff**: say what gets harder, slower, less magical, or more explicit.
7. **Landing**: leave the reader with the useful sentence they can carry.

## Mapping

- Want becomes hook plus recognition.
- Need becomes friction plus turn.
- Get becomes mechanism plus tradeoff plus landing.

## Gate

The journey passes when:

- the first sentence creates a reason to keep reading
- the pain arrives before the solution
- the mechanism is concrete
- the tradeoff is visible
- the post sounds authored, not outlined

If the draft would need `Want`, `Need`, or `Get` headings to make sense, the journey is not ready.
