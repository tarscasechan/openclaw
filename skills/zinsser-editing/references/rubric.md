# Zinsser Editing Rubric

Use this when polishing prose.

## Pass 1: Structure
- Can it be framed as want / need / get?
- Is the lead honest and fast?
- Does the piece open curiosity before explaining?

## Pass 2: Meaning
- What is the point?
- Does each paragraph move the reader?

## Pass 2: Clutter
- Cut throat-clearing.
- Cut repeated claims.
- Cut empty intensifiers.
- Cut passive voice when it hides the actor.
- Cut abstract nouns when a verb will do.

## Pass 3: Voice
- Keep the human cadence.
- Keep one memorable line if it earns itself.
- Avoid sounding like an essay about writing.

## Stop rules
- Stop if the next edit only changes tone, not clarity.
- Stop if the prose starts feeling performed.
- Stop if shortening would damage rhythm.

## Preferred signals
- verbs do the work
- sentences stay short unless the idea needs a breath
- specifics beat generalities
- the reader understands quickly and keeps moving
